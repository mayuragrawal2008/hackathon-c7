# Move 2 — The Hypothesis (locked before any application code)

**Track A: The Concept Check**
**Name:** Mayur Agrawal
**Status:** Refined after the three Move 1 sessions, still before a single line of app code.

## The claim
A 100x learner who passes a normal "did you understand" check — they can state or
define a systems concept (interface, API, backend, storage) — will still **fail to
derive the *why*** (why it must exist, what breaks without it) under one sharp
follow-up, more often than not. Naming the exact gap and handing them **one
reasoning follow-up** (not the answer) lets them close it in the same sitting.
Understanding is scored on the **causal "why," not on technical vocabulary** — a
correct plain-language derivation counts as a pass.

## The three numbers (Move 5 = 2 users, at least 1 cold)

- **Result that proves me right:** both users can talk about the concept, **fail
  the "why" on first try**, and after I name the gap + one follow-up, **derive the
  why in their own words** (jargon optional).

- **Result that means I imagined a problem nobody has:** both users **derive the
  why cleanly on first try**, no follow-up needed. The gap I bet on is not real.

- **Kill-number:** if **1 or 0 of the 2** users shows a real before→after
  "why"-close, I stop defending the idea and write: **"my hypothesis was wrong."**

## The Eddie clause (failure mode I own, on purpose)
A learner who derives the *why* **without** jargon (e.g. "the backend is the muscle
and organs, the internet is just the skeleton") is a **PASS**, not a gap. Flagging
missing vocabulary as a gap is the false-positive I am building against. I caught
myself doing exactly this in Move 1 Person 3 (Eddie) — grading on the technical
words I expected to hear instead of on whether he understood. The tool must judge
derivation of the why, never the presence of technical terms.

## Honesty check
- **What would change my mind:** Move 1 already pushed on it — Hansh derived
  everything (no gap), Eddie derived without words (false gap). If both Move 5
  users behave like them, the bet is dead and I will report it.
- **Is the kill-number real:** yes. "1 or 0 of 2 closes" is a line that can
  genuinely fire — Hansh-type and Eddie-type learners are common in the cohort.
  It is not rigged to never trigger.

> These numbers are locked here before any application code. They will not be
> edited after Move 5 data is collected. Moving them after the fact is an automatic fail.
