## The claim
A 100x learner who passes a normal "did you understand it" check — meaning they
can *define* a systems concept (interface / API / frontend-backend / storage) —
will still **fail to derive it from first principles under one sharp follow-up**,
more often than not. Naming the exact gap, and handing them one targeted
follow-up, lets them close it within the same sitting.

## The three numbers

- **Result that proves me right:** Of the people tested, the majority (≥ 60%)
  who can define the concept cannot derive it under the first sharp follow-up —
  AND, once the gap is named, the majority of those who failed can then derive it.

- **Result that means I imagined a problem nobody has:** Most people (≥ 60%)
  who can define the concept *also* derive it cleanly under the follow-up with no
  prompting. The gap I am betting on does not exist.

- **Kill-number:** If **1 or fewer of the 2** Move 5 users shows a real
  before/after derivation gap that closed (i.e. fewer than half show the effect),
  I stop defending the idea and write: **"my hypothesis was wrong."**
